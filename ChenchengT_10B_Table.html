Concrete

 | |

5% of Global C02 emissions

 | |
Concrete is used to create hard surfaces which contribute to surface runoff that
may cause soil erosion, water pollution and flooding.
The presence of some substances in concrete, including useful and unwanted additives,
can cause health concerns due to toxicity and (usually naturally occurring) radioactivity
 |
|

Cement

 | |

8% of Global C02 emissions

 | |
Bags of cement routinely have health and safety warnings printed on them because not only
is cement highly alkaline, but the setting process is exothermic[releases heat].
 |
|

Steel

 | |

10% of Global C02 emissions

 | |
The industrial processes involved in steel production release greenhouse gases,
particulate matter[Generic term to classify air pollutants comprising suspended particles in the air],
and VOC [volatile organic compounds; Many VOCs are toxic and can cause cancer, mutations,
and/or other serious health problems.] into the air..
 |
|

House Heating and Cooling

 | |

28% of Global C02 emissions

 | |
Almost 25% of the energy produced worldwide is used to heat and cool homes and commercial buildings.
According to the World Green Building Council around 28% of global CO2 emissions come from building operations,
due to the energy used for heating, cooling and power. HVAC systems affect the environment in several ways.
They consume significant household energy—about 48%, according to the U.S. Department of Energy
