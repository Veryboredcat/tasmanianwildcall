Environments the devils can be found

The Tasmanian Devil is native to Tasmania, Australia, and is found across all habitats on the island, including coastal heathlands, open dry sclerophyll forests, and mixed sclerophyll-rainforests. They are also present in urban outskirts and have been reintroduced to mainland Australia in a sanctuary in New South Wales.

What is its niche?

The Tasmanian Devil is a nocturnal scavenger and opportunistic feeder (an animal that eats whatever food is available, without a strong preference for specific types of food). Its main role in its ecosystem is consuming the decaying flesh of dead animals, which helps in nutrient cycling and controlling disease spread.

  1. Predators:

    Eagles, wolves, and quolls mostly target small/young devils.

  2. Prey:

    The devils sometimes hunt insects, amphibians, reptiles, birds, and mammals while scavenging.

  3. Competitors:

    Other scavengers and carnivores in the ecosystem like quolls, eagles, and foxes.

What the devils do for their ecosystem

Through eating dead animals, the devils are reducing the maggots and insects out keeping the bush and farm hygiene.


Tasmanian devils act as natural regulators of the ecosystem. By competing with or deterring other invasive species like feral cats, they help reduce the predation pressure on vulnerable native species.
According to statistics: There are 42% less feral cats in places where Tasmanian devils are higher in population.
